Ca' d'Oro was built by the Contarini family, one of the longest-living families in Venice, which gave 7 doges to the republic, and had many palaces built all over Venice. When you look at the social network of marriages in the republic, this family has a high degree of self-loops, marriages between different members of the family.

Let me tell you about the Contarinis

This is the palace Contarini degli Scrigni e Corfù; they are a single complex, the Corfù palace is on the right, the other on the left. It's a palace complex belonging to one of the branches of the family, called "degli Scrigni", or "the chests", as in "treasure chests". The Scrigni palace is the work of Scamozzi, one of the most important architects after Palladio and Longhena. He proposed architecture as a complex science, more than an art. Look at the number of windows: hexafora in Ca d'Oro, tetrafora (four windows) here. This was proportional to the size of the halls in the piano nobile: these guys were rich. And abundant. As a matter of fact, biggest number of fora is here, at Ca' Foscari, with a 8-window polifora.https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ca%27_Foscari_(palazzo)

It seems to set the record straight (Giustinian vs. Dolfin; Loredan vs. Pisani)

This is Rialto as it could be, projected by Palladio and painted by Guardi. We are looking at the study of eigenvector centrality as it should be, once we take into account all data that can be actually used to compute it. Self-loops work arithmetically as expected: those families with a certain number of self-loops get a boost, which might make them rank higher. The Giustinian have 8 self-marriages, the Dolfins 4, are the Giustinian better "centered" along the axes of communication? Well, the Giustinian had only 1 doge; they were an "evangeliche" family; they had different "rami" (San Moisè, Budelle d'Oro; these last seemed to be the most successful); the Dolfins seem to have many religious members and thus were probably not as successful. Did the inclusion of self-loops put them in the right order? The Giustinian seem to be marginally more influential than the Dolfins; the Pisani more than the Loredans. But this would need a lot of additional research.
